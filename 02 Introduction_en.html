Four Non-Tibetic Tibeto-Burman Languages in Tibet

The Chamdo Regional Gazetteer (《昌都地区志》) (西藏昌都地区地方志编纂委员会, 2005, p. 819) documents that there are three dialects in Chamdo that are not intelligible with Kham Tibetan. They are Zésong Huà (则松话) in the County of Chagyab (བྲག་གཡབ་རྫོང, 察雅县, cháyǎ xiàn), Rúměi Huà (如美话) in the County of Markam (སྨར་ཁམས་རྫོང, 芒康县, mángkāng xiàn), and Dōngbà Huà (东坝话) in the County of Zogang (མཛོ་སྒང་རྫོང, 左贡县, zuǒgòng xiàn). Suzuki & Nyima (2016) first reported these non-Tibetic Tibeto-Burman languages in Chamdo: 'Bo skad and sMar skad. Nyima & Suzuki (2019) identified four language islands: Lamo, gSerkhu, Larong sMar, and Drag-yab sMar.

Larong sMar is spoken in the border area of the Counties of Zogang and Markam, with 17,000 speakers. Drag-yab is spoken in the County of Chagyab by approximately 20,000 speakers. These two languages correspond to sMar skad and are being documented by Mr. Zhao Haoliang, a PhD student affiliated with INALCO (Institut national des langues et civilisations orientales, National Institute for Oriental Languages and Civilizations).

However, the number of speakers of Lamo is much smaller, with only 3,000, mostly in the County of Zogang. The situation of gSerkhu is even more critical, with only 400 individuals living in the County of Zayü (རྫ་ཡུལ་རྫོང, 察隅县, cháyú xiàn), in the Prefecture of Nyingchi (ཉིང་ཁྲི་གྲོང་ཁྱེར, 林芝市, línzhī shì). Given the threat to these languages, gSerkhu and Lamo require immediate documentation and comprehensive description. Due to its proximity to border areas, conducting long-term research in the region where gSerkhu is spoken is difficult. Therefore, this database focuses on the documentation and description of Lamo.

Two Varieties of Lamo

The Lamo language is primarily spoken in the two townships of Dobbar (སྟོབས་འབར་ཤང་, 东坝乡, dōngbà xiāng) and Zhonglinka (ཀྲུང་གླིང་ཁ་ཤང་, 中林卡乡, zhōnglínkǎ xiāng).

The Lamo language consists of two dialects, known as Lamo and Lamei, designated /la.mo/ and /la.mɛ/ respectively, as identified by Suzuki & Nyima (2021, p. 26). These two variants are mutually intelligible, with differences in phonetics and vocabulary.

According to local inhabitants, the Lamo dialect is spoken in four of the seven villages in Dobbar. These villages are Jūnyōng, Géwǎ, Pǔkǎ, and Jiābà. The Lamei dialect, on the other hand, is spoken in three of the thirteen villages in Zhonglinka: Shízìkǎ, Luòbā, and Ruòbā.
